=== RateMyDorm Freshman Dorm Ranking (June 2026) ===
1. Maple Hall (West Campus) — 14 reviews. Clean private bathrooms; kitchens on every floor with
   regular cleaning service. Close to engineering classes and the Ave; lots of study space
   (two kitchens, four study rooms per floor). Corner rooms have Seattle skyline views. Noted as
   somewhat expensive, and it gets warm in spring/early fall — bring a fan.
2. Poplar Hall (West Campus) — 11 reviews. Prime location near the Ave; elevator/lounge areas
   with Space Needle views.
3. McMahon Hall (North Campus) — 13 reviews. "Cluster style" suites (share a bathroom and living
   space with a small group) make it very social. Bathrooms cleaned twice weekly, on-site gym,
   main-floor patio, ample storage, balcony views of Mt. Rainier and Lake Washington. Downsides:
   older "legacy" look, no kitchens, occasional building smell, uneven room temperatures.
4. McCarty Hall (North Campus) — 11 reviews. Spacious rooms but thin walls; near Greek Row, so
   potential party noise.
5. Terry Hall (North Campus) — 6 reviews. Smallest hall, which fosters community; notable rooftop
   deck; farthest from the main academic core.

=== Maple Hall — detail ===
Praised for modern design, comfort, and convenience: strategic location near engineering classes
and the Ave, abundant study spaces, two kitchens, and four study rooms per floor. Rooms (especially
corner rooms) offer skyline views and are noted as spacious for doubles or singles, with ample
storage, loft beds, and clean private bathrooms. Main criticisms: relatively expensive, and warm
in spring/early fall.

=== McMahon Hall — detail ===
The cluster layout (shared bathroom and living space with a small roommate group) creates a social
atmosphere that's good for making friends. Praised for affordability, convenient north-campus
location, ample in-room storage, private bathrooms, a main-floor patio, and an on-site gym. Balcony
views of Mt. Rainier and Lake Washington stand out despite the building's older look. Common
complaints: no kitchen facilities, an occasional smell, and varying room temperatures.

=== Common patterns across reviews ===
- West Campus (Maple, Poplar) = newer/better amenities, but a ~10-minute walk to most classes.
- North Campus (McMahon, McCarty, Terry) = closer to academics, more traditional/social, but some
  older buildings and potential Greek Row noise.
- McMahon's cluster suites are the most social option; trade-off is noise and no kitchen.
- Request a corner or high room for the best views and space.
