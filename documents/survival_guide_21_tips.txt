21 Tips for Making the Most of Your First Year at UW

1. Get to know your campus. Use UW Campus Maps and explore different areas to find study spots, views, and comfortable places on campus.

2. Take advantage of your U-PASS. Use unlimited bus and light-rail access to explore Seattle's neighborhoods and discover hidden treasures.

3. Speak up in class or quiz section. Engage with the material through questions and discussion to improve understanding and stand out to instructors.

4. Form study groups. Connect with classmates — especially those in your FIG (First-year Interest Group) — to study together and reinforce learning.

5. Sit in the front of the classroom to help you pay attention. Positioning yourself prominently helps maintain focus during lectures.

6. Be yourself! Maintain your authentic personality to connect genuinely with the people around you.

7. Build relationships with your professors. Seek out instructors or TAs to discuss coursework and get academic support.

8. Find a balance. Prioritize rest, self-care, and downtime alongside academic and social commitments.

9. Use your unlimited Google Drive storage space. Store files securely through your UW email account so they're accessible anywhere.

10. Try to resolve conflict with your roommate through direct communication rather than avoiding the issue.

11. Get to know your RA. Build a relationship with residential staff who can offer valuable perspective and support.

12. Attend campus events. Participate in activities hosted by student organizations, ASUW, and Housing & Food Services.

13. Cheer for the Huskies! Attend free sporting events like volleyball, gymnastics, and rowing to socialize and take a break from studying.

14. Ask for help. Use campus resources including CLUE, the writing centers, and subject-specific tutoring.

15. Have backup classes ready, with SLNs (Schedule Line Numbers) noted in advance, so you can quickly adjust your schedule during registration.

16. Take photos. Capture moments with friends and memorable campus experiences for future reflection.

17. Write it down. Document memories and meaningful quotes in a journal or notes for later.

18. Make healthy choices. Balance dining options, exercise on campus trails, and stay hydrated.

19. Don't stress if you lose touch with some of your high school best friends. This is normal during college transitions.

20. And don't worry if you don't find your college best friends right away either. Meaningful connections develop over time.

21. Fail forward. Learn from mistakes and setbacks; visit the UW Resilience Lab for resources and inspiration from others' experiences.
